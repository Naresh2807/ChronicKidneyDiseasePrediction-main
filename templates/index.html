CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) means your kidneys are damaged and can't filter blood the way they should. The disease is called “chronic” because the damage to your kidneys happens slowly over a long period of time. This damage can cause wastes to build up in your body. CKD can also cause other health problems.

CHALLENGES

Complications from CKD produce system-wide symptoms. Some of the more common include anemia, heart disease, high calcium levels, and bone disease. There are five stages of CKD beginning with mild damage in Stage 1 to complete failure in Stage 5.

MOTIVE

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) is a life-threatening condition that affects nearly 14% of the world's population, predicting it with the utmost accuracy allows people to be diagnosed and treated at the lowest possible cost and risk.

POTASSIUM

People with chronic kidney disease (CKD) have a high risk for hyperkalemia, due in part to the effects of kidney dysfunction on potassium homeostasis. It is reported that hyperkalemia frequency is as high as 40-50% in people with chronic kidney disease .

ALBUMIN

Albumin provides the body with the protein needed to both maintain growth and repair tissues.If your albumin level is good, fluid will move more easily from swollen tissues into the blood, where it can then be removed by the dialyzer.

HEMOGLOBIN

Low hemoglobin can be a marker for the severity of kidney disease. However, in people with CKD, the mean GFR of those with low hemoglobin was 44.7 ml/min per 1.73 m2, compared with a mean GFR of 49.2 ml/min per 1.73 m2 in people with higher hemoglobin.

RBC COUNT

When your kidneys are damaged, they produce less erythropoietin(EPO), a hormone that signals your bone marrow to make red blood cells. With less EPO, your body makes fewer red blood cells, and less oxygen is delivered to your organs and tissues.

SUGAR

High sugar levels in the blood can cause kidney vessels to become narrow and clogged. Without enough blood, the kidneys become damaged and albumin (a type of protein) passes through these filters and ends up in the urine where it should not be. Nerves in your body.

PUS CELL

When the body detects an infection, it sends neutrophils to destroy the fungi or bacteria. During this process, some of the neutrophils and tissue surrounding the infected area will die. Pus is an accumulation of this dead material. Many types of infection can cause pus.

SERUM CREATININE

A serum creatinine level of greater than 1.2 for women and greater than 1.4 for men may be an early sign that the kidneys are not working properly. Several factors can cause high creatinine levels. As kidney disease progresses, the level of serum creatinine in the blood rises.

APPETITE

Anorexia is common in patients with CKD and may result from alterations in orexigenic (appetite stimulating) and anorexigenic (appetite inhibiting) hormones, accumulation of metabolic waste products in the body in kidney failure and abnormal taste.

PEDAL EDEMA

Bilateral pedal edema is the most common symptom in chronic kidney diseased patients. It occurs due to the loss of functioning of the kidney. This may lead to fluid accumulation in the body and also an accumulation of excretory products or waste products increases in blood.

HYPER TENSION

Hypertension is both a cause and effect of CKD and affects the vast majority of CKD patients. High blood pressure can make your kidneys worser. Control of hypertension is important in those with CKD as it leads to slowing of disease progression as well as reduced CVD risk.
